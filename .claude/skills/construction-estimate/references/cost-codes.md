# Cost codes

CSI MasterFormat-95 sixteen divisions, plus a local Div 0 (bidding) and Div 17 (changes).
Taken from the Codes sheet of the 2033 E. 7 workbook, which is the house standard.

Codes are stored numerically with a `0.00` display format, so 2.10, 6.10 and 9.10 read as
2.1, 6.1 and 9.1 if you parse them as numbers. Watch for that when sorting.

If a scope item doesn't fit an existing code, raise it rather than inventing a code.

## Per-job repurposing

Codes get renamed per project where a division is unused. On 2033 E. 7: 10.1 became
"Miscellaneous items", 10.4 added "Miscellaneous glazing", 11.3 became "Outdoor kitchen",
13.1 "Fire pit", 13.6 "Home theatre", 16.1 "General", 16.6 added "and heat".

That's acceptable, but record the renaming in the estimate so the client document and the
Sage export agree.

## Divisions that behave unusually

**Div 8 carries no labour.** Doors and windows are supply-only; installation hours live in
6.9 and 6.10. If you put install labour in Div 8 the export to Sage will double-count.

**Div 0 prices to zero by design** on most jobs. It exists to hold bidding and contract
conditions, not cost.

**Div 1 scales with schedule, not with area.** Job site costs and supervisory spread across
the project duration. Change the months-to-complete and Div 1 must move. On 2033 E. 7 it was
12% of trade cost across a 12-month programme.

**Div 17 is for change orders only**, coded exactly `C1`…`C20`, lower or upper case, never
`C-2` or `C 2` — the claim formulas match on the exact string.

## Ogden trade codes

The earlier Ogden interior renovation used a flat, non-CSI trade code list (DEMO, SS, FL,
ML, FRM, LWC, PL, PLF, HVAC, SP, EL, ELF, INS, LV, FP, DRY, TM, TL, PNT, CAB, CNT, TILE,
INTL, FLR, HDW, GLAS, AP, WC, SPF, CLN, GL, SF, FEES, EXT, FS, MSM, REN, COR, TRV, STR,
TST), grouped as Preparation/Preliminaries, Mechanical pre-drywall, Finishing Trades,
Caisley labour, Other Expenses.

Estimate in CSI. That grouping is still a reasonable shape for the *client-facing* rollup on
a smaller interior job, where 16 divisions would be more structure than the job warrants.
